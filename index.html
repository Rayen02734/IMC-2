An anime[1],[2], or animé[2] (アニメ?)Listen to pronunciationⓘ, also sometimes called japanime[note 1] or japanimation[note 2], is an animated series or animated film from Japan, often adapted from a manga. It is the diminutive of the word animēshon (アニメーション?), itself a transcription of the English animation[1],[2],[3]. While the earliest known Japanese animation dates back to 1917[4] and a good number of original cartoons were produced in the following decades, the anime character and style developed during the 1960s (notably thanks to the work of Osamu Tezuka) and became popular outside Japan during the 1970s and 1980s. Anime, like manga, enjoys a large audience in Japan and is easily recognizable worldwide. Distributors can distribute anime through television channels, video, in theaters, or even streaming.
